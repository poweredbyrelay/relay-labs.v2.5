Effective Date: September 26, 2025

This Data Processing Addendum ("DPA") forms part of the Terms of Service, Subscription Agreement, or other written contract between Relay Labs LLC, a New Jersey limited liability company ("Processor"), and the customer entity identified in the applicable Order Form ("Controller").

1. Roles of the Parties

1.1 Controller / Processor. For purposes of applicable Data Protection Laws, Controller is the data controller (or business) and Relay Labs LLC is the data processor (or service provider).

1.2 Definitions. "Data Protection Laws" means all laws and regulations relating to privacy, data protection, and processing of personal data, including GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, and any implementing legislation. "Personal Data" has the meaning given in such laws.

2. Processing of Personal Data

2.1 Subject Matter and Purpose. Processor shall process Personal Data solely to provide the Services described in the Agreement.

2.2 Nature and Types of Data. Personal Data includes identifiers (names, emails, phone numbers), billing details, communication content, call/SMS logs, and usage analytics. No sensitive categories are intentionally collected.

2.3 Duration. Processing shall continue for the term of the Agreement, subject to retention provisions in Section 7.

2.4 Instructions. Processor will process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Controller, unless otherwise required by law.

3. Security Measures

Processor shall implement and maintain reasonable and appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect Personal Data, including encryption in transit and at rest, access controls, authentication, logging, vulnerability management, and employee training. Detailed measures will be provided upon reasonable request.

4. Breach Notification

Processor shall notify Controller without undue delay and in any case within 72 hours after becoming aware of a confirmed Personal Data Breach. Notifications shall include available details regarding the nature of the breach, affected data, and remedial actions. Processor shall cooperate with Controller in managing breach response.

5. Subprocessors

5.1 Authorization. Controller authorizes Processor to engage subprocessors necessary to provide the Services.

5.2 Initial List. Subprocessors as of the Effective Date:

  • Twilio Inc. (telecommunications services)
  • Stripe, Inc. (payment processing)
  • Google LLC (infrastructure and analytics)
  • OpenAI, L.L.C. (AI processing)

5.3 Updates. Processor will notify Controller at least ten (10) business days prior to adding or replacing a subprocessor. Controller may object in writing. If the parties cannot resolve the objection, Controller may terminate the affected Services without penalty.

5.4 Flow-Down. Processor shall ensure subprocessors are bound by data protection obligations no less protective than those in this DPA.

6. Audit Rights

6.1 Upon request, Processor shall provide Controller with information necessary to demonstrate compliance, including third-party audit reports (e.g., SOC 2 Type II).

6.2 Controller may conduct remote or on-site audits with 30 days' prior written notice, during normal business hours, and in a manner that does not disrupt operations. Controller shall bear all audit costs unless a breach is confirmed.

7. Return and Deletion of Data

Upon termination of the Services, Processor shall, at Controller's option, return Personal Data to Controller within 30 days or securely delete/irreversibly anonymize it within 90 days, unless law requires longer retention.

8. Data Subject Rights and Cooperation

Processor shall assist Controller in fulfilling obligations to respond to data subject requests (DSARs), including rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection. Processor shall also provide reasonable assistance with regulatory inquiries, impact assessments (DPIAs), and consultations with supervisory authorities.

9. International Transfers

Personal Data may be transferred to and processed in the United States and other jurisdictions. For transfers from the EEA, UK, or Switzerland, the parties agree that the EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) form part of this DPA, with Controller as the data exporter and Processor as the data importer.

10. Liability and Indemnity

10.1 Alignment with Agreement. Liability under this DPA is subject to the limitations and exclusions of liability set forth in the Agreement. Aggregate liability shall not exceed amounts paid in the preceding twelve (12) months.

10.2 Carve-Out. These limitations shall not apply to liability arising from Processor's gross negligence or willful misconduct.

10.3 Indemnification. Controller shall indemnify Processor for claims, penalties, or fines arising from Controller's violation of Data Protection Laws, including unlawful instructions or non-compliant communications.

11. Term and Termination

This DPA shall remain in effect for the duration of the Agreement and shall survive termination to the extent Processor retains Personal Data for legal compliance or record-keeping.

12. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

This DPA shall be governed by the laws of the State of New Jersey, excluding conflict-of-law rules. Any disputes shall be resolved through binding arbitration in accordance with the Agreement, including waiver of class and representative actions.

14. Miscellaneous

14.2 Entire Agreement. This DPA, together with the Agreement, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties concerning data processing.
